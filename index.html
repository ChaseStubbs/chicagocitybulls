Draft Context
Pick #4 + Splitter = Rare Alignment
Whoever Graham selects at #4 walks into a development-first environment with a coach who has proven he can unlock young forwards. Graham + Splitter isn't a win-now setup — it's the best possible ecosystem for a 19–20 year old to grow into a star over 4 years.

Splitter's Pace vs. Graham's Defense-First Mandate
The one tension: Graham built the Pelicans as an elite defensive unit (slow, physical, half-court). Splitter runs top-10 pace. The bet is that Splitter's Popovich discipline applies the brakes when needed — and that Chicago's length (Okoro, Williams, Buzelis, Giddey) is long enough to defend in transition.

🚨 Priority #1 — June 29 Deadline: OKC must decide on team options for Lu Dort ($17.7M) and Isaiah Hartenstein ($28.5M) before July 1.
